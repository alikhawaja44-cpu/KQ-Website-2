01 Overview

What Is a UK Spouse Visa?

The UK Spouse Visa is the primary immigration route under Appendix FM of the Immigration Rules — available to partners of British citizens, settled persons, and certain qualifying sponsors.

Per Appendix FM E-ECP.1.1, both parties must be aged 18 or over, have met in person, and be in a genuine and subsisting relationship.

Unlike visitor or work visas, this is a settlement route — applicants progress through structured stages toward British citizenship.

Common Mistake Alert

Sponsors with limited leave (Skilled Worker, Student, etc.) cannot sponsor Spouse Visa applications. Their partners require Dependant visas instead.

Can I switch from Visitor to Spouse Visa?

No — you must leave the UK and apply from abroad

The Immigration Rules explicitly prohibit switching from a Standard Visitor visa to any settlement route.

Myth-busting: The COVID-19 concession expired August 2022 and has not been renewed.

04Relationship

Proving Your Relationship Is Genuine

The Home Office assesses relationship genuineness through four key pillars per Family Migration Guidance 8.1:

Cohabitation

Official documents at same address

Communication

WhatsApp / call logs during separations

Financial Ties

Joint accounts, transfers, shared bills

Future Plans

Intent to live together permanently

The 6-Piece Rule

Provide 6 documents spread across 2 years. Quality over quantity — each should show a different aspect of your relationship.

Acceptable Documents:

  • Joint tenancy agreements
  • Individual utility bills at same address
  • Statutory declarations from family/friends
  • Travel booking confirmations
  • Wedding photos (10–15 spanning timeline)

Common Questions:

Use individual documents at the same address plus statutory declarations from family or friends who can confirm your cohabitation.

10–15 high-quality photos spanning your entire relationship timeline, showing different occasions and with different people.

06Housing

Accommodation Requirement

Housing Standards

Must meet Housing Act 1985 Part X standards — no overcrowding.

Room Calculation Rules:

  • Children under 10 can share regardless of sex
  • Children 10+ must have separate rooms if opposite sex
  • Maximum 2 persons per bedroom

Exclusive Occupation

Can be a single room in a shared house — but it must be solely for the couple's use, not shared with landlord or other tenants.

Exceptional Circumstances (10-Year Route)

The 10-year route applies only where refusal would result in:

  • Unjustifiably harsh consequences for the applicant or their family
  • Insurmountable obstacles to family life outside the UK
  • Compelling compassionate factors requiring UK residence

Citation: Appendix FM EX.1
